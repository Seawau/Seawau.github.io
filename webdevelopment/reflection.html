Reflection

Why I Decided on Psychology

Despite the fact I have been in school for quite some time, it feels a bit unreal to be a senior in college. As I take classes, I have the occasional thought that I could have taken another route–I still can. Maybe in another life I might have decided to stick with becoming an author. If I had found certain interests sooner, I would have gone into fashion and business, or dance. When I tell people I’m a psychology student, they say something along the lines of, “Why would you want to do that?” I understand the reaction–it’s dangerous territory. I chose it anyway because I want something concrete. In my relationships with peers and growing experiences of the world, there is a bountiful number of unanswered questions, struggling people, and uncertainties.

I can choose to do something I find fun, but I quickly realized that life boils down to more than that. Fun doesn’t always stick around if you don’t have a firm understanding of your life, yourself, and others. Especially the ones we care about. It would be akin to trying to run on sand. The more of the world I take in, the more daunting it seems. There may always be peers I cannot fully understand, connect with, or struggle with something unseen. I am not ignorant enough to believe I can solve every problem, or that I need to, but I want to be equipped to handle any issues that come my way–or address ones that affect my peers.

This is why I pursue understanding human behavior. Mental struggles are some of the hardest to understand because they are not tangible–and college students are rife with them, no matter the degree. The more psychology courses I take, the more intrigued I get. While I am no master, I have learned about childhood development, veterans psychology, and behavioral addictions. With each course I take, I am grateful again and again that I have decided to stick with psychology. They give me a new perspective to understand people who don’t even understand themselves. I understand just a little bit more about these people that I feel I couldn’t make connections with before–who felt understood because they lacked the knowledge too.

I have learned that humans are a social species, whether someone says otherwise or not. Even so–I certainly am, despite my introvertedness. I am delighted by the idea that I can see people in a new light. That I can offer some comfort of being understood because I have also learned people crave that. You can tell by the way students gravitate towards clubs and student organizations, Greek towns–like-minded people that they can find a place with. I have never lived on campus, but in-person classes and club opportunities have made some of the best memories I have had. The connections I have with my peers have made my college days about more than just “going to class.” They lit up the room, they made me look at studying as more than just an obligation. I want to understand these people so I can protect this happy slice of my world.

June 20, 2026 - Gracie Hulett
